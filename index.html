// 02 · ABOUT

Datasheet.

I'm a final-year Electronics & Communication Engineering student who got hooked the first time a breadboard circuit actually worked on the first try — and has been chasing that feeling ever since. Most of the time it doesn't work on the first try, and that's where the real learning happens.

My work sits at the intersection of hardware and firmware: designing analog front-ends, writing register-level embedded C, and getting protocols like CAN, UART, and I2C to actually shake hands with each other. I care about signal integrity as much as code quality — a beautiful driver means nothing on a noisy rail.

Outside coursework, I spend evenings in the lab fixing what I broke in the afternoon, reading datasheets like bedtime stories, and slowly building a personal library of modules I can reuse on the next build.
